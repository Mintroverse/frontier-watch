Obsolescence check

Is the in-house work still the right bet?

Editorial assessment — judgment, not source data

Short answer: still the right bet — this week the market priced the layer we already own.

Venture capital paid up for the thesis our stack embodies: a governed data layer above the systems of record, with agents on top. Meanwhile the adoption data says most AI programs fail on change management, not technology — which favors the small-gated-pilots posture over any big-program pivot. Nothing this edition argues for buying big; two things argue for executing faster on what is already queued.

Validated by this edition

  • The context-layer bet is now market-priced: Auger's round and DeepFabric's GA both monetize the layer above the ERP — the architecture our Lakehouse + semantic model estate already implements in miniature.
  • The reality-check numbers (S04) vindicate the pilot discipline: measured baselines, gated autonomy, workflow redesign first — the exact shape of the SADA-plus-gates template, now with an adoption gate (M18).
  • Standard rails held: five days to the MCP final with the ecosystem writing operational playbooks; the post-final validation work is queued in the right order — hold, validate, then pilot (M11) — and our own platform ships first-party connectors (S09).

Watch — could overlap or absorb

  • The pure-play layer could commoditize from above: if mid-market operators adopt Auger-class platforms, the differentiation of an in-house operating layer shrinks — watch for mid-market pricing and packaging of these platforms.
  • Acumatica's silence continues: R2 brings AI Document Intelligence, but still no first-party MCP or AI Units pricing news — the integration-dependency question stays open a fourth cycle (M13, tracked since E03).
  • P7 is a cost risk, not a tech risk: AI-driven freight demand raises our own landed costs regardless of our AI posture — the exposure view (M17) is the cheap hedge.
